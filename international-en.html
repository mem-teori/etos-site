ETOS – A Framework for Systemic Tension in Complex Decision Environments

This page is written in a more neutral and professional register for international partners, EU environments, foundations, researchers and policy actors.

Download EN one-pager Download DE one-pager
Core

ETOS is a conceptual and analytical framework designed to examine how tensions emerge between intentions, actions, outcomes and lived experience within complex decision-making systems.

GovernancePolicy, implementation and public impact
HealthcareAutonomy, relation and long-term consequence
AI systemsProxy metrics, legitimacy and responsibility
What ETOS does

Not only decisions — but the structures that shape them

Rather than treating decisions as isolated events, ETOS focuses on the structural relationships that shape them — including institutional rules, human experience and normative expectations.

Governance systems

When intention and implementation drift apart

ETOS can be used to examine cases in which policy aims and actual public impact no longer align.

Healthcare and psychiatry

When clinical decisions meet lived reality

The framework is useful in environments where autonomy, patient experience and long-term consequences cannot be reduced to simple guidelines.

AI and algorithmic systems

When optimisation loses contact with the real world

ETOS offers language for the relationship between proxy metrics, actual impact, legitimacy and responsibility.

Why now

Complexity, abstraction and scale are widening the gap

Between what systems are designed to achieve, what they actually do, and how they are experienced in reality.

Research positioning

Decision theory, ethics, governance and human–AI interaction

ETOS is positioned at the intersection of decision theory, ethics, governance and human–AI interaction. The framework builds on the premise that many real-world challenges are not caused by a lack of rules, but by unresolved tensions between competing structures.

A conceptual version of the framework has been submitted to SCAI 2026, and the project is currently open for interdisciplinary collaboration.
