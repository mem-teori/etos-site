What does ETOS do?

Overview before action

When decisions become difficult, it is rarely due to a lack of rules — but because different considerations pull in different directions.

ETOS helps you see the tension clearly enough to act responsibly.

  • Makes tensions visible — not just felt
  • Creates overview before action — not panic
  • Gives direction — not an answer
  • Builds learning over time — not just point-in-time assessment
The process

How it works

Four steps from situation to decision — and on to learning.

1

Situation

You describe a concrete situation — what is at stake, who is involved, what pulls in which direction.

2

Analysis

ETOS makes tensions and conflicts visible. Calculates divergence, ethical pressure and room for action.

3

Overview

You see what pulls in each direction — and which state the situation is in. Not an answer, but clarity.

4

Decision

You act with greater awareness. The situation is logged. Patterns emerge. You learn over time.
