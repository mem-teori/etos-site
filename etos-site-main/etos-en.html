Governance-native decision infrastructure

ETOS

Inspectable, constitutionally governed recursive self-correction architecture

ETOS is designed as a governance layer for high-risk decision systems — and as a practical testbed for alignment problems on the path toward AGI. It does not treat decisions as isolated outputs, but as movements through structures, consequences, and feedback.

High-risk governance Recursive correction Inspectable alignment Historical accountability
System structure

Decisions move through systems

ETOS follows how decisions propagate down through structures — and how consequences, friction, and correction move back through the system.

Parliament

Defines the normative direction and formal mandate. This is where what counts as legitimate action is framed at the highest level.

Government

Turns political direction into executive pressure, priorities, and practical momentum. Here the abstract becomes actionable.

Central Administration

Translates direction into rules, procedures, and institutional logic. This is the layer where governance becomes administratively real.

Regions

Carries decisions into large operational systems such as healthcare, where complexity, scale, and coordination become decisive.

Municipalities

Implements decisions close to everyday life, where systems meet citizens directly and where consequences become concrete.

Citizens

Where decisions are actually lived, felt, resisted, or carried. ETOS makes this layer visible instead of treating it as downstream noise.

Why it matters

ETOS does not treat decisions as answers

Instead of optimising directly toward output, ETOS works with tensions, drift over time, and persistent ethical consequences. That makes it possible to reject technically efficient changes if they distort the system's normative purpose.

AGI perspective

A practical alignment testbed

ETOS is not AGI. But it is a practical example of inspectable recursive self-correction, where a system can say no to its own improvement and show why.
