AGI-BABY · Baseline v1

A working developmental system with three regimes

AGI-BABY is not AGI. But it is no longer just a score machine. It is a dynamic system that can grow, collapse, and recover — and do so differently depending on the environment. The most important theoretical finding:

"Stabilisation is not the same as recovery."

🟢 Stable

Credible life cycle. Can reach infant/toddler phase, fall back, and recover again. Collapse via coherence-erosion. Recovery is possible and varied.

🟡 Unstable

Vulnerable but not deterministic. Some runs reach infant phase, others collapse early. Pause-lock occurs — but not always.

🔴 Overload

Correct extreme state. Never progression. Permanent pause-lock. Collapse via suppression. No recovery possible.

What is being investigated

The central questions across the tracks

The tracks are independent — but investigate variants of the same core question about freedom, pressure, and recovery in systems at different levels.

ETOS · Recursive governance

Can a system learn under governance?

ETOS investigates whether a system can correct itself without sliding around its own constitutional frameworks.

AGI-BABY · Recovery

When is stabilisation not enough?

AGI-BABY shows that a system can stabilise — and still lose coherence. Recovery requires active mechanics, not just dampening.

ETOS · Drift

Can normative movement be detected early?

Drift, velocity, acceleration, and oscillation are used as practical signals that a system is moving away from its purpose.

The Conductor · ETOS-P

Can a human have a governance layer?

The Conductor tests whether ETOS principles can be transferred to human decision energy — with tension models and governance modes.
