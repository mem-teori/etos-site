The problem we work with

In healthcare, difficult decisions are rarely difficult because rules are missing. They are difficult because considerations pull in different directions — and because they must be documented, justified and learned from afterwards.

The situation

  • Patient refuses treatment. Risk is high. Trust is low.
  • Documentation takes time. Decisions are made under pressure.
  • Who decided what — and why — is difficult to reconstruct.
  • Institutional learning rarely happens systematically.

What ETOS offers

  • Makes the tension visible — not just the situation
  • Documents the decision with justification and ethical context
  • Full audit trail — who, when, in what state
  • Pattern recognition across cases over time

Human oversight as an architectural principle

execution_allowed = False. The system does not make decisions. It makes them visible, documented and learnable — so the human who decides can do so responsibly.

Five decision modes

ETOS classifies situations in five states — as signals that orient, not categories that lock:

Normal

Tensions are manageable. Standard procedure is sufficient.

Requires review

Tensions are real. Action is possible — but with a conscious cost.

Emergency

High divergence. Action is needed now. Document the dilemma explicitly.

Incompatible

Two considerations cannot both be met. The system clarifies — does not recommend.

Constitutional veto

Action would violate inviolable principles. The system says stop.
