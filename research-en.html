Research principle

Open research — in the spirit of Bohr

M.E.M. Research follows a simple research principle: knowledge becomes stronger when it is shared, tested, and challenged.

In the spirit of Niels Bohr, openness is not decoration, but a condition for trust, criticism, and further development. The research tracks on this page are therefore shared with clear knowledge boundaries, explicit reservations, countercases, and status markers.

M.E.M. is not presented as finished truth. It is a working field where hypotheses must be able to meet other perspectives.

"Real co-operation between nations on problems of common concern presupposes free access to all information of importance for their relations."

B · Simulation Lab — ongoing hypothesis testing

Simulation Lab —
path dependence and freedom in decision systems

Five foundational experiments exploring the Freedom Metric: how early choices in a decision system narrow or widen future room to act. Simulation-based — hypothesis-generating, not hypothesis-confirming.

Measurable

The Freedom Metric

The degree of freedom in a simulated decision system can be made measurable — not just described qualitatively.

Strong finding

Early bias → path dependence

Early bias in the system creates strong path dependence — later correction becomes more expensive the longer it takes.

Third outcome

Metastable low-freedom states

The system can land in a third state — neither collapse nor healthy operation, but a stable, low-freedom state that does not self-correct.

Intervention type

Relational → narrative intervention

Interventions that act on relations and narrative reduce collapse more effectively than purely structural measures.

Under development

Meta-Recovery Lag

A measure of how long it takes a system to recognise and respond to its own loss of freedom — not yet frozen.
