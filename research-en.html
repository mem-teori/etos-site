Research Corner · public lab

A place to follow how ETOS is tested, challenged, and shared

Not just a page of results — but a place where the work can be followed in motion.

The Research Corner is intended as the public lab around ETOS: demos, simulations, working papers, platform updates, and eventually a place where others can start or connect their own projects.

Open the demo →Read about the platform ↓

In the spirit of Niels Bohr

The idea is simple: scientific work and understanding should be shareable. Not everything must be opened at the engine-room level, but development, arguments, and findings should be readable, discussable, and extendable.

Why this page exists

Because ETOS should not only be shown — it should be possible to follow it

When you link from posts, discussions, or collaboration outreach, there should be one place where people can understand the direction, the maturity, and the experimental work around ETOS. The Research Corner is intended to be that entry point.

For readers and the curious

Access the current demo, see how ETOS describes tensions, and follow how the platform develops over time.

For potential collaborators

Use this page as the starting point if you want to discuss a pilot, a working paper, a simulation, or a project that could grow through ETOS.

Next step

Over time this can become a portal for shared exploration

The aim is not only to publish, but to create a place where people can follow along, think along, and perhaps start a project of their own from within the Research Corner.
