Start here

ETOS is a place to begin when things do not hold together.

It is built for situations where something may look correct on paper, while lived experience tells a different story.

What?
A framework for observation, reflection and learning.
Why?
To make responsibility and consequences visible.
How?
First understanding, then depth, then use.
In short

ETOS helps make visible what otherwise gets lost between layers.

Many difficult situations are not just about one wrong decision. They are about tensions between what was intended, the system that shaped it, what was actually done, and how it was experienced.

ETOS was developed to make those tensions visible, so they can be understood, examined and turned into learning.
