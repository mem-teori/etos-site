What is Maxi?

An extension of Bankeren — for those who cannot put it into words

Bankeren is the sensory anchor in the Children's Universe — the lamp and the teddy that give feelings a place to be. Maxi is Bankeren made responsive.

Where Bankeren is a calm, static presence, Maxi is an active one. It senses the room. It registers touch, sound, light and movement — and responds with its own bodily expression: a turn, a colour, a gentle vibration.

It is not therapy. It is not diagnostics. It is a tool for returning to one's own body — especially in the situations where words are not enough, and people can feel too large.

The four states

Maxi speaks with its body, not with words

The M.E.M.I. core continuously selects one of four states based on what the sensors register. The state is expressed physically — with head, light and touch.

Probe · searching 🔍

Something is unknown

The field is unstable or new. Maxi turns quickly, amber light pulses.

Head: fast, searching LED: warm amber · fast pulse Vibration: rhythmic
Sample · attentive 🫧

Something draws attention

Something in the room requires attention. Slow turn toward the source, blue light.

Head: slow, leaning toward you LED: blue · gentle pulse Vibration: soft hum
Stabilize · calm 🌿

The field is stable

Calm. Maxi is heavy and still, green light with almost no movement.

Head: heavy, downward LED: green · barely pulsing Vibration: none
Hold · rest 🌑

Epistemic idle

Nothing to learn. Maxi is completely still, faint grey light, no movement.

Head: neutral, still LED: grey · faint Vibration: none
M.E.M.I. as the brain

Not rule-based — tension-based

Maxi does not follow a script. The M.E.M.I. core continuously calculates a tension field from the sensors and selects the action that best stabilises the system — exactly as a living being would.

Sensing

The field is felt

Microphone, motion sensor, touch and light are fused into one signal every half second.

Decision

Tension is calculated

M.E.M.I. calculates drift, volatility, recoverability and uncertainty — and selects the best operator.

Expression

The body responds

Servo, LED and vibration translate the decision into physical behaviour. Not a response to you — but with you.

What Maxi senses

Four sense channels

Maxi hears, sees, feels and registers — not what you say or do, but the intensity of what is present in the room.

🎤

Sound

Microphone measures amplitude only — not what is said. Maxi hears the intensity of the world, not its content.

MEMS microphone
👁

Movement

PIR sensor detects movement in the room. Not who — only that there is disturbance or calm.

HC-SR501 PIR
✋

Touch

Pressure-sensitive sensor registers contact. Touch is weighted slightly higher than the other channels.

FSR 402
☀️

Light

Light sensor registers day/night and sudden changes in ambient light.

Who is Maxi for?

Where words are not enough

Maxi is not mainstream. It is a tool for the situations and contexts where traditional approaches fall short.

Crisis and trauma

When language disappears

In acute crisis situations and trauma treatment, a quiet, responsive presence can help return to the body — without requiring language or explanation.

Autism and ADHD

Sensory regulation

Children and adults who need sensory input as a path to regulation can use Maxi as a predictable, responsive object that does not judge.

Isolation and loneliness

A presence that is there

For those living alone or in periods of extreme isolation, Maxi can offer the simplest thing: something that reacts, without demanding anything in return.

Maxi is not — and does not replace

a therapist or clinician
a surveillance device
a diagnostic instrument
a substitute for human contact
an autonomous system that acts
an app or a screen

Maxi is:

a physical tool for sensory regulation
a bridge to the human — not a replacement for it
governance-native: no data collected, no external transfer
