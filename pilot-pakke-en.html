What is a pilot

90 minutter.
Én situation.
Virkelig læring.

A pilot is not a course or a lecture. It is a concrete session where ETOS meets a real situation — your situation.

You do not need to prepare anything. Just bring your situation.

✗

Not a lecture on theory

✗

Not a solution to your situation

✗

Not therapy or counselling

✓

A concrete session with your real situation

✓

Overview of tensions you could not see clearly

✓

Clearer decisions — not necessarily easier

✓

Your feedback helps make ETOS better

Forløbet

90 minutes — minute by minute

The pilot follows a fixed structure — but starts from your situation.

0–10
Min 0–10 Introduction

Kort introduktion til ETOS og hvad vi gør i dag. Ikke teori — bare kontekst nok til at komme i gang.

"I am testing a tool that helps understand difficult situations — not solve them quickly."
10–25
Min 10–25 Your situation

Du fortæller. Hvad der er svært. Hvad der er på spil. Ingen struktur endnu — bare situationen som den er.

"What are you dealing with right now?"
"What is difficult?"
25–45
Min 25–45 ETOS analysis

Vi udfylder ETOS-parametrene sammen. Risiko, menneskelig faktor, relationel tillid, tidspres. ETOS viser divergens, etisk pres og handlingsrum.

Risk · Autonomy · Relational trust · Time pressure
45–65
Min 45–65 Understanding

Her sker det vigtigste. Du ser din situation på en ny måde. Ikke fordi ETOS har svaret — men fordi strukturen synliggør noget du ikke kunne se tydeligt.

"What surprises you?"
"What makes sense?"
"What becomes clearer?"

✦ This is where the magic happens

65–80
Min 65–80 Decision

Fra forståelse til handling. Ikke store planer — bare ét næste skridt der er mere bevidst end det forrige.

"What do you do now?"
"What do you do differently?"
"What do you stop doing?"
80–90
Min 80–90 Your feedback

Det vigtigste for ETOS' videreudvikling. Din ægte reaktion — god og dårlig — er mere værdifuld end ros.

"Did it help?"
"What was missing?"
"What was difficult to understand?"
Din rolle som deltager

The pilot makes no demands for preparation. Bring your situation — raw, unstructured and unfinished. That is exactly what we work with.

✗

✗

No "right" situation

All situations are welcome — large and small

✓

Bring a real situation

Something that is genuinely on your mind right now

✓

Be honest in feedback

Your criticism is more valuable than your praise

"The facilitator is not the expert with the solution — but the mirror that makes the structure visible. Those are two very different things."

What we look for

Not "was it perfect?" — but this:

🧠
Did they understand it?
Did the ETOS language make sense without prior theory?
🌊
Did it create calm?
Did they experience the complexity becoming manageable?
🔍
Did it bring clarity?
Did they see something they could not see clearly before?
🧭
Did it change something?
Did they leave the session with a more conscious next step?
Typical reactions — these are success criteria
"That makes sense"
"I can see it more clearly"
"It is difficult… but good"
"I knew it — but now I can see it"
"I am not the problem"
